Games

Please bear in mind I wrote, illustrated and coded these before starting the coding course and there are definitely things I'd do differently now, including some responsive design and sprucing up the GDPR box.

Election Run® is a politics-themed game in which players pick a candidate to play and run for election using a simple random card system. Play it here.

FameGrafter® was inspired by the ZX Spectrum Codemasters Classic 'Rockstar Ate My Hamster' and puts the player in the hotseat of a talent agency, assigning his performers to a different project each week in the worlds of film, music and more. The random show name generator actually works quite well. Play it here.

Artificial Untelligence® was an attempt to ease my unease over the rapid rise of AI in the form of a very basic spoof, featuring an image generator, horoscopes, unhelpful therapist and unhelpful support chatbot, all working off simple random generators. Give it a go here.

Arachnospiracy: The Halton Fear™ is a very simple text-based adventure game and something of a protest at some of the clustered poor-quality public realm and dereliction. Play it here.

I've also written a game that I'm hoping to publish as a standalone download purchase once I've settled on a better general brand name. At the moment it's working title is Project V.

If anyone would be interested in developing any of the games as a commercial project and making an offer, collaborating or hiring, get in touch. No dodgy characters.
